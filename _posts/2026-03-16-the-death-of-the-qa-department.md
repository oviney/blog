The traditional QA department is undergoing a profound transformation, as embedded quality engineering (QE) practices become the norm in software development. According to Global Market Insights, the global QA industry is projected to grow by 6% annually, indicating that quality assurance roles are not only persisting but thriving. However, this growth masks a deeper shift towards integrating quality into every step of the software development lifecycle.

## Embedded Quality Engineering Takes Centre Stage

Once isolated in their own silos, quality assurance units are increasingly being woven into the fabric of software development processes. This shift is driven by the need for integrated and continuous oversight, with 60% of enterprises globally having embraced embedded QE teams, according to the Capgemini World Quality Report of 2025. Such teams are not merely adjuncts to development; they are intrinsic to it, ensuring that quality is not an afterthought but a constant presence.

As companies pivot towards embedded QE practices, the traditional role of QA testers faces significant evolution. Forrester Research anticipates a 20% reduction in traditional QA roles by 2030. This reduction does not denote a decrease in quality obligations but rather a redistribution of those responsibilities across development teams. Developers, supported by embedded QE specialists, are increasingly expected to ensure the quality of their code from inception to deployment. This transformation heralds an era where QE expertise is becoming a core skill for all developers, comparable to coding itself.

## Employment Trajectories Defy Automation Expectations

Despite fears of job displacement due to automation, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics forecasts a 7% growth in the number of software quality assurance analysts and testers in the U.S. from 2024 to 2034. This projection defies the widespread apprehension that automation would eradicate QA roles outright. Instead, it paints a picture of an industry adapting to technological changes rather than succumbing to them.

This paradox of growth amidst structural change can be partly explained by the complex demands of modern software environments which require specialised human oversight even as automated systems gain prominence. Studies by Forrester reveal that automated testing tools, while efficient, often lack the nuance required for comprehensive quality analysis, necessitating human intervention for interpretation and oversight.

As the chart shows, QE roles experience a higher growth rate compared to traditional QA roles, underscoring the increasing importance of QE skills.

## Rethinking Quality in a Digital Era

The movement towards embedded quality engineering is also a reflection of broader changes in how value and success are measured in the digital economy. Companies now face immense pressure to deliver flawless user experiences consistently, making quality engineering not just a technical requirement but a strategic imperative.

Moreover, the integration of QE into development processes aligns with the agile and DevOps methodologies that dominate contemporary software environments. These methodologies emphasize rapid iteration and continuous delivery, concepts that are fundamentally at odds with the static checkpoints of traditional QA. An embedded QE approach ensures that quality is part and parcel of this fast-paced rhythm, allowing for real-time feedback and adjustment.

## The Future of Quality Engineering

The standalone QA department is becoming obsolete as embedded QE practices redefine how quality is managed in technology-driven enterprises. This interdisciplinary approach will define the future of software development. As companies embrace more sophisticated technologies—such as AI-driven test automation and continuous integration systems—the demand for skilled quality engineers who can navigate and harness these tools will only grow. Thus, the landscape of quality engineering is expanding rather than contracting.

Ultimately, businesses that effectively integrate quality engineering into their core operations will be best positioned to thrive amid the relentless demands of the modern marketplace.
